Redesigns

The Wii went through 3 main redesigns The first was the Wii RVL The main change to the Wii was the RVL- processor Which juts add more combability HDMI for newer TV's. After this remodel Nintendo released a cost reduced variant of the original Wii this Model was known as RVL-101. But more commonly known as the Family module. It was released later in the consoles life span and removed almost all GameCube functionality including the port for the GameCube controllers. And memory cards found with original model. IT also doesn't include a stand. Nintendo announced the redesign in august 2011. For the original Wii model which was slowly discontinuing. The new units where priced at $149.99 instead of $200 the original Wii's where going for. A special bundle of the Console was released in blue ion certain Stores across North America. The Wii Mini was third and final addition to Wii line of consoles . The Wii mini is a smaller, Wii with a top-loading disk drive instead of the side loading one prier Consoles had. In addition GameCube Support and removes Wi-Fi connectivity and online connectivity entirely. along with the removal of the SD card slot. And 480p and component for video output. The Wii mini released for a price $99 in late 2013. The Wii mini release date at the end of the Wii's product life costed the Wii mini to flop greatly.
